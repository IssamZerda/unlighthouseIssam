Driving business growth for a DevOps and software development outsourcing company 

Fondative’s competitive edge lies in its ability to seamlessly handle foundational DevOps and software development aspects, speeding up project delivery for its clients and freeing up their teams for added-value tasks. 

Beyond word of mouth, Fondative’s website is the principal tool with which it markets its services and acquires new business in and outside France — yet the company evolved significantly since building its original website.

Fondative selected Zerda Digital to create an entirely new website. Working with leadership, we took this website revamp to the next level, restructuring the service offer, rethinking the information architecture and creating new UI and content to bolster business development actions across marketing channels.
  • Challenges

Outdated material — As Fondative evolved and expanded, its website didn’t follow suit, undermining its brand image and hindering business development actions.

Information architecture — The legacy website’s convoluted information architecture meant it had to be reworked from the ground up, taking into account Fondative’s increased capabilities. 

Ill-suited content — Complex and emphatic writing styles, more akin to older school print practices, focusing excessively on the WE and lacking in client benefits. 

Visual identity — Embody Fondative’s Japan-inspired principles. Print-led brand identity guidelines include outmoded, at times conflicting design elements. 

Double duty — The new website should raise potential clients’ awareness of Fondative’s capabilities while serving as a hub for the company’s Marketing team, putting business development material at their fingertips. 

  • Solutions
Conduct workshops with Leadership and Marketing to reflect on the company's evolution, refine brand positioning, define communication axes and tone of voice, and underscore strengths. 
Discovery and SWOT analysis
Building on Discovery and SWOT phase takeaways, outline and hone site map and information architecture, mindful of target audiences and Marketing team's respective user experience. 
New information architecture
Build on the revamped information architecture to create high-fidelity wireframes for the entire website, test navigation, and establish content production guidelines. 
User experience (UX)
Adopt a two-stage approach: the Fondative team will leverage its industry knowledge on first drafts, and the Zerda Digital team will edit the final content from an outside marketing expert's perspective. 
Upgraded content
Streamline legacy branding visual elements and adapt design language for the web. Incorporate new iconography, micro-interactions that shift users' focal points, and ambiance videos optimized for page performance.
